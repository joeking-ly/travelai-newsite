The State of AI in early 2024: Gen AI adoption spikes and starts to generate value

The 2024 McKinsey Global Survey on AI highlights the transformative impact of generative AI across industries, including travel—with adoption surging and organizations seeing real value from gen AI.

TA
TravelAI Team
Published Jul 30, 2024 • 4 min read

The 2024 McKinsey Global Survey on AI highlights the transformative impact of generative AI across various industries, including travel. With AI adoption surging, the travel sector is well positioned to benefit from the advancements and applications of gen AI. Here are the key findings from the report and how they relate to the travel industry.

1. Adoption Rates and Impact

  • The overall adoption of AI has increased to 72% globally, with significant uptake in the travel and hospitality sector. Organizations in this industry are leveraging AI to enhance customer experiences, optimize operations, and improve decision-making processes.
  • The use of gen AI specifically has surged, with 65% of organizations now regularly utilizing this technology, up from 33% in the previous year.

2. Business Functions and Use Cases

  • Customer Experience: Gen AI is revolutionizing customer interactions by enabling personalized marketing, real-time customer support, and tailored travel recommendations. This technology can provide travel companies with the ability to offer customized itineraries, improve booking experiences, and enhance customer satisfaction.
  • Operations Optimization: AI is being used to streamline operations, from automated check-ins and smart room allocations to predictive maintenance of facilities. This leads to cost reductions and more efficient use of resources.
  • Sales and Marketing: The adoption of AI in marketing and sales has more than doubled, allowing travel companies to identify and prioritize high-value leads, create targeted marketing campaigns, and generate content that resonates with travelers.

3. Value Creation

Travel companies are seeing significant benefits from AI adoption, including both cost savings and revenue growth. AI-driven analytics can help optimize pricing strategies, improve inventory management, and enhance demand forecasting. The report highlights that human resources within the travel industry have experienced cost decreases due to AI-driven efficiencies in recruitment, training, and employee management.

4. Investment Trends

The travel industry is increasingly investing in gen AI and analytical AI technologies. Many organizations are allocating more than 5% of their digital budgets to these innovations, with expectations for continued investment growth over the next three years. Customization of gen AI solutions is becoming crucial for travel companies to gain a competitive edge, as off-the-shelf models may not fully meet the unique needs of the sector.

5. Risk Management

The primary risks associated with gen AI, such as inaccuracy and data privacy concerns, are particularly relevant to the travel industry, where customer trust and data security are paramount. Leading travel companies are adopting best practices to mitigate these risks, including robust data governance, early risk reviews, and comprehensive testing protocols.

6. Challenges and Implementation

Travel companies are facing challenges in integrating gen AI capabilities, particularly in terms of data management and agile operating models. Effective implementation of AI solutions often requires overcoming hurdles related to data quality, governance, and integration. Despite these challenges, organizations are finding that AI deployment can be achieved relatively quickly, with many projects taking less than four months to go live.

Best Practices for the Travel Industry

  • High-performing travel companies are leveraging AI in multiple business functions, particularly in customer service, marketing, and operations.
  • Investment in talent and training is critical, with companies building skill sets necessary to effectively implement and manage AI technologies.
  • Emphasizing risk mitigation, they ensure AI applications are aligned with regulatory requirements and organizational values.
  • These organizations focus on developing customized AI solutions that address specific industry needs, thereby creating a competitive advantage.

Overall, the rapid adoption and integration of generative AI are set to transform the travel industry, driving significant value through enhanced customer experiences, optimized operations, and improved decision-making. By strategically investing in and managing AI technologies, travel companies can unlock new opportunities and maintain a competitive edge in the evolving market.

For the full report, see McKinsey: The State of AI.

The Bottom Line

Early 2024 was the inflection point where gen AI stopped being hype and started generating real value—travel included.
