Eating My Way Through Vietnam's Street Food Scene

From pho to banh mi, my culinary tour of Vietnam was a revelation. Here's where to find the best street food in Hanoi, Da Nang, and Saigon.

LC
Lucas Chen
Published Feb 8, 2026 • 9 min read

Vietnam's street food is legendary — and overwhelming. I wanted to eat like a local without missing the classics or the hidden stalls. TravelAI suggested spots by city, time of day, and my tolerance for heat and crowds, so I had a map instead of a random walk.

Hanoi, Da Nang, Saigon

In Hanoi, pho and bun cha were non‑negotiable; the app pointed me to places that balance authenticity and accessibility. In Da Nang, I followed suggestions for mi quang and banh xeo near the river. In Saigon, banh mi and com tam led the way, with a few coffee and dessert stops that I'd never have found on my own.

The Verdict on the Ground

Street food in Vietnam is best when you have a mix of must-dos and curveballs. AI gave me both — and my stomach thanked me.

The Verdict

You could spend a lifetime eating through Vietnam and still miss gems. With a little structure from recommendations that understood "street food" and "three cities," I hit the highlights and still found my own favorites.
