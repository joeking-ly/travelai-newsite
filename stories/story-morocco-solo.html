Solo Female Travel in Morocco: What I Wish I Knew

My month backpacking through Morocco taught me more than I expected. Here are the lessons, tips, and incredible moments from my solo adventure.

AR
Ava Rodriguez
Published Feb 10, 2026 • 10 min read

Solo female travel in Morocco comes with a lot of questions — and a lot of opinions. I spent a month there with a mix of guidebook research and AI-curated suggestions for riads, guides, and routes that other solo travelers had found safe and rewarding.

Preparation and Reality

What I wish I'd known: dress codes and bargaining are real, but so is hospitality. I used the app to find female-run guesthouses and vetted day tours, which cut down on guesswork and left more energy for the medinas and the desert.

Highlights That Delivered

From Fes to the Sahara, the recommendations that considered "solo" and "first time in Morocco" made a visible difference. I had a list of spots that were busy enough to feel safe but not so touristy that they lost their edge.

The Verdict

Morocco as a solo female traveler is doable and incredible. Go with good prep, trusted recommendations, and an open mind — you'll leave with more than photos.
