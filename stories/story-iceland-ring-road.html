Iceland Road Trips

The Ultimate Iceland Ring Road Itinerary

Ten days circling Iceland's Route 1 taught me that the journey really is the destination. Waterfalls, glaciers, and geothermal pools await.

NT
Nina Taylor
Published Feb 5, 2026 • 11 min read

The Ring Road is Iceland's greatest hit — 800-plus miles of otherworldly landscapes. I had ten days and a rental car. TravelAI helped me break the loop into manageable days, with suggested stops for weather, light, and my preference for fewer crowds where possible.

Highlights That Hit

From Seljalandsfoss and Skógafoss to Jökulsárlón and Mývatn, the suggestions matched my pace: early starts for popular spots, flexible alternatives when the weather turned, and a few detours that weren't on every list but were worth the extra drive.

Ten Days, One Loop

You can rush the Ring Road in a week or stretch it to two. With a day-by-day outline that considered driving time and light, I never felt rushed — and I still had time for hot springs, local meals, and the occasional unplanned stop.

The Verdict

Iceland's Ring Road deserves the hype. Doing it with a bit of structure — and suggestions that adapted to conditions and my interests — made it the trip I'd been imagining for years.
